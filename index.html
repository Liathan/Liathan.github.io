Introduction: The importance of trees

Ecobenefits from a tree

The trees represent one of the first places of natural shelter that are sought outdoors in hot weather, or even a meeting point for young and old. Their growth and protection are of fundamental importance for the well-being of man and the territory. There are numerous local authorities that are particularly active in emphasizing the importance of planting more and more trees, especially in cities. They bring numerous benefits that are easily received by each of us: benefits both on the environmental front, such as the reduction of temperatures, of particulate matter (PM 2.5), and the drainage of the land, and on the ornamental front, of beautification of the city. There is also a kind of flip side, where branches, or sometimes whole trees, fall to the ground, roots breaking sidewalks, or leaves on sidewalks in the fall that could cause people to slip. But these negative aspects can all be limited by proper care and monitoring of the trees themselves.

The main objective of this project will be to understand which species are the most widespread, those that bring greater benefits and how they are arranged in the territory of the municipality of Trento. To do this, we will use a set of data provided in open-source mode on GitHub (TrentoTreeMap Project), which we will analyze to extrapolate all the information necessary to best represent the current situation of the trees in the Trento area. We will also aim to estimate in economic terms the benefits brought by the trees in the area, or how much man would have to spend to achieve similar goals, also depriving himself of the beauty of different tree species.

First assignment: Comparing categories

In this first block of visual representations, we want to compare the species of trees that are present on Trento's territory. We will start showing the top 15 tree species in term of abundance (absolute values). Then, we will pick the top 5 ones, plus an "Others" category which includes all the other tree species, and we will show you their abundance in each circoscrizione of Trento's territory. This comparision between circoscrizioni will be carried out using four different visualizations:

  • a stacked barchart, using the abundance of each category;
  • a small multiple stacked barchart, using again the abundances;
  • a percentage stacked barchart, which will use the percent values of the abundance of each species;
  • and a wafflechart, which will also use the percent values.

Second assignment: Distributions and correlations

Now, we have two different goals: firstly, to understand the distribution of some measurements of the trees and, then, to discover possible correlations between them and the specific tree species. We will begin showing the distribution of some measures (like height, diameter, canopy size, and leaf area) on the whole dataset of trees via histrograms. Then, we will discover the distribution of those variables using boxplots about the top 5 tree species on the territory. After, we will skip to a two-dimensional representation using scatterplot: we will discover possible correlations between the selected measures for the trees and the kilograms of CO₂ that they can absorbe during a year. The data-points will be colored with respect to the corresponding top 6 tree species. After, we will split this scatterplot in a small multiple scatterplot where we can find six different plots, one for each of the top 6 classes. At the end, we can find an interactive bubblechart in which we compare a measurement of the tree with the CO₂ that it can absorbe, and with its canopy size.

Third assignment: Maps

In this third block of visual representations , we want to visualize the map of Trento divided by neighborhoods , and extract some characteristics for each of them. The fields we want to compare and visualize in real time are : Tree abundance , density (the amount of neighborhood space covered by trees' canopy cover ) , oxygen production and the trees position.

  • a choropleth map , using the abundance of trees in each neighborhood;
  • a choropleth map , using the density ;
  • a choropleth map , using the oxygen production per tree;
  • a dot density map , for the visualization of tree's position.
  • a dot density map , for the visualization of tree's position divided by top six tree type.
